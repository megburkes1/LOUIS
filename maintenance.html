Maintaining LOUIS

Regular maintenance is essential to keep LOUIS operating smoothly. Below are some key maintenance tasks to ensure LOUIS stays in top condition.

1. Solar Panel Maintenance

LOUIS relies on solar power for energy, so it's important to replace the solar panel when the efficiency is lagging .

  • Replace the solar panel every 20-25 years, unless it breaks sooner than that.
  • For reference, the solar panel was purchased in November of 2024.

2. Battery Care

The battery is an essential component for LOUIS's operation. Regular checks will ensure it functions optimally.

  • Check the battery voltage regularly to ensure it is charging properly.
  • Keep the battery terminals clean and free from corrosion. Use a wire brush to clean any build-up.
  • Ensure that the battery is properly connected and securely mounted in place.
  • Replace the battery every 2-5 years.
  • For reference, the battery was purchased in January of 2024.

3. Motor and Sensor Maintenance

LOUIS’s motor and sensors should be inspected regularly to ensure they are working efficiently and accurately.

  • Check the motors for any visible damage, and ensure they are securely fastened to avoid wear or malfunction.
  • Inspect all sensors (e.g., proximity, temperature, etc.) for any dirt or blockages.
  • Calibrate sensors periodically to ensure accurate data collection.

4. Software and Firmware Updates

Keep LOUIS’s software and firmware up-to-date to ensure it runs optimally and is equipped with the latest features.

  • Check for updates regularly through the control panel or associated app.
  • Update both the onboard software and any external software or apps used to control LOUIS.
  • Backup data before performing any updates to prevent loss of important information.

5. General Cleanliness

General cleanliness is important for the long-term health of LOUIS. Dust and dirt can affect various components, including motors, sensors, and wiring.

  • Keep the rover's body clean by wiping it down with a damp cloth.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, as they may damage the materials.
  • Ensure there are no obstructions around the moving parts to prevent wear and tear.
